# Discharge AI

AI-assisted discharge coordination application for an NHS hospital.

This README is intended to be used by Cursor, Cursor Agent, or another code-generation agent as the product and implementation specification for building the MVP.

## 1. Product Summary

Discharge AI is a clinically governed discharge coordination platform for NHS ward teams.

The system should help doctors, nurses, pharmacists, therapists, discharge coordinators, and site teams identify which patients are discharge-ready, what is blocking discharge, who owns each action, and what documentation still requires review.

The AI should support the discharge process by summarising patient information, detecting missing information, generating draft discharge plans, drafting documentation, and tracking blockers.

The AI must not independently decide that a patient is fit for discharge. Final approval must remain with an authorised human clinician.

## 2. Product Vision

Build a ward-level discharge command centre that follows this workflow:

```text
EPR patient data
+ structured doctor/nurse questions
+ free-text clinical context
        |
        v
AI discharge agent
        |
        v
Draft discharge plan
        |
        v
Task, blocker, and status tracking
        |
        v
Low-risk administrative automation
        |
        v
Final human clinical approval
        |
        v
Discharge documentation and follow-up workflow
```

The system should make it obvious:

- Which patients may be discharged today.
- Which patients are blocked.
- What is blocking each discharge.
- Who owns each action.
- What needs clinical approval.
- What has been generated by AI.
- What evidence supports the AI output.

## 4. Non-Negotiable Safety Principles

The application must be human-in-the-loop.

The AI may:

- Summarise clinical context.
- Identify missing information.
- Suggest tasks.
- Draft discharge plans.
- Draft discharge summaries.
- Draft patient advice.
- Draft GP handover text.
- Flag blockers.
- Suggest escalation.
- Show uncertainty.
- Show source evidence where available.

The AI must not:

- Independently decide that a patient is medically fit for discharge.
- Submit final discharge documentation without approval.
- Change medication orders.
- Send external clinical communications without approval.
- Override clinician judgement.
- Hide uncertainty.
- Invent facts.
- Present suggestions as clinical decisions.
- Act without audit logging.

Every AI output must be reviewable, editable, attributable, and auditable.

### 6.2 Structured Doctor/Nurse Questions

The app should provide dynamic forms. Questions should be grouped by discharge domain.

#### Medical Readiness

- Is the patient medically fit for discharge today?
- Are there unresolved clinical issues?
- Are any investigations pending before discharge?
- Is consultant review required?
- Is follow-up required?
- Are red flags and safety-netting instructions documented?

#### Home and Care Needs

- Can the patient return to their usual residence?
- Does the patient live alone?
- Is a package of care required?
- Has the package of care been confirmed?
- Are carers or family aware of the plan?
- Is home equipment required?

#### Therapy and Mobility

- Has physiotherapy cleared the patient?
- Has occupational therapy cleared the patient?
- Is the patient safe with transfers?
- Is the patient safe with stairs?
- Is the patient safe with toileting?
- Is the patient safe with feeding?
- Are mobility aids required?

#### Transport

- Does the patient need hospital transport?
- Can family collect?
- Is ambulance transport required?
- Is the destination home, care home, rehab, or another hospital?

#### Medicines

- Has medicines reconciliation been completed?
- Are TTO/TTA medications prescribed?
- Has pharmacy screened the discharge prescription?
- Does the patient need counselling on new or high-risk medicines?
- Are anticoagulants, insulin, opioids, antibiotics, steroids, or other high-risk medicine plans clearly documented?

#### Family Communication

- Has the patient been informed?
- Has family or next of kin been updated?
- Are there concerns or objections?
- Is capacity documentation required?
- Is best-interests documentation required?

### 6.3 Free-Text Clinical Context

The interface should allow short free-text entries from doctors and nurses.

Example:

```text
Patient lives alone. Daughter can collect after 5pm. Awaiting OT stair assessment. TTOs not yet screened.
```

The AI should use this free text to update the proposed discharge plan and blocker list. Consequential actions must remain reviewable and auditable.
